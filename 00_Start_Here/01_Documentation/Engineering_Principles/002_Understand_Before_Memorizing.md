# Engineering Principle #002

## Principle

Understand the concept before trying to memorize it.

---

## Meaning

Memorization fades.

Understanding lasts.

If I understand why something works, I can rebuild the details even if I forget them.

---

## Why It Matters

Programming is solving problems—not recalling syntax.

Understanding allows me to adapt to new languages and technologies.

---

## Example

Instead of memorizing what a virtual environment is, I learned why it exists and what problem it solves.

---

## Key Takeaway

Understanding creates long-term knowledge. Memorization only creates short-term recall.
